THE FINANCIAL SERVICES WE ARE AUTHORISED TO PROVIDE TO YOU

Our financial services business involves providing general financial product advice, dealing in over-the-counter (OTC) financial products such as derivatives and market making services in relation to OTC derivatives (including but not limited to contracts for difference and margin foreign exchange contracts) that we issue on behalf of the Authorizer. The Authorizer acts as principal in all transactions Switch executes with you in respect of the financial products offered by us and is the counterparty to all trades. Your funds will be held in the Authorizer’s segregated client trust account.

We are authorised by Royal ETP LLC to provide the following financial services to retail clients and wholesale clients.

(a) provide general financial product advice for the following classes of financial products:
i. deposit and payment products limited to: A. basic deposit products; B. non-cash payment products; i. derivatives; ii. foreign exchange contracts; and securities; (b) deal in a financial product by: i. issuing, applying for, acquiring, varying or disposing of a financial product in respect of the following classes of financial products: A. derivatives; and B. foreign exchange contracts; and ii: applying for, acquiring, varying or disposing of a financial product on behalf of another person in respect of the following classes of products: A. deposit and payment products limited to: 1. basic deposit products; 2. non-cash payment products; B. derivatives; C. foreign exchange contracts; and D. securities; and (c) make a market for the following financial products: i. foreign exchange contracts; and ii. derivatives; to retail and wholesale clients.

Switch may receive fees and other benefits from the financial products that it issues on behalf of the Authorizer to you. These may take the form of spread and/or commission payments. Unless otherwise stated, all fees, charges, commissions and benefits disclosed in this FSG are inclusive of goods and services tax (GST).

OTC Financial Product Fees

The fees, costs and charges for OTC financial products are disclosed in the product disclosure statement and Market Information Sheets for those products. General

If you owe an account to Switch and you have not paid that amount, Switch may charge interest on that outstanding amount at the rate set out in or on the basis described in the Account Terms or, if not specified or described, at the rate equal to Switch’s prevailing debit interest rate determined by it from time to time. Switch may charge a fee for printing and postage of confirmations if you have the ability to receive electronic confirmations but have chosen not to do so. We may also charge reasonable amounts for additional reports or communications required by you. These fees will be advised to you prior to you requesting this information.

Administration Fees

Additional fees may be charged for transactions and services, including but not limited to international transfers, bank cheques and additional account administration fees. Switch may charge fees for certain administrative services which occur in the course of your dealings with us.

Reductions & Waivers

Switch may in its absolute discretion reduce or waive the minimum deposit amount, the minimum account balance or interest rates on debit balances, electronic trading platform charges, administration charges, SMS service fees, fees (including royalties or fees for third-party services) or transaction charges, for individual clients or for classes of clients, for any length of time, with or without conditions.

Third-Party Charges
The fees and charges payable by you to Switch may differ from the fees and charges payable by Switch to its third-party service providers from time to time.

SMS Service Fee
Should you request the delivery of correspondence regarding your account via the Short Message Service (”SMS”), you must pay an SMS Service Fee to Switch.

The SMS Service Fee will be due and payable to Switch upon delivery of a SMS and will be debited from your Account at the end of each month. The SMS service fee is US$0.55. This fee is charged per SMS and is inclusive of GST.

Introducing Broker Fees

If you have been introduced to Switch by an introducing broker or referrer, they are required to disclose to you, the remuneration and benefits paid to them. As we are unable to ascertain the exact remuneration at this time, you may request particulars of their remuneration (including commission) or other benefits paid to them within a reasonable time after you are given this FSG and before any of the financial services offered herein are provided to you.

Remuneration and Benefits of Authorizer

The Authorizer receives a ticket fee of up to USD$20 per mil in notional trades it hedges from Switch clients, paid by Switch.

The Authorizer may also receive a benefit where they have taken on the risk of your trade in the form of a B-book strategy and the trade moves in their favour.

DISCLOSURE OF CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

Trading with Switch and the Authorizer carries an automatic risk of conflicts of interest because the Authorizer is acting as principal in its positions with you; Switch, as authorised representative, sets the price of the contracts; and because the Authorizer might be transacting with other persons at different prices or rates. You can reduce the risks of unfavourable pricing or opaque pricing by monitoring Switch’s contract pricing and monitoring the underlying market.

REPRESENTATIVE AGENTS & INTRODUCING BROKERS

The fees and charges described in our PDS, and in this FSG, apply where you accept the financial services and products offered by Switch on behalf of the Authorizer. If you deal with Switch through an Introducing Broker, that Introducing Broker may have its own fees and charges.
